# PorkChop Seller Onboarding Packet (Workforce + Continuing Education)

**Purpose:** Give sellers exactly what to say when prospecting K-12 CTE, community colleges, technical colleges, and workforce/continuing-ed programs.

**Built from Charles Gallo's requested framework:**
- ICP
- Persona
- Value prop to that persona
- How that persona leverages PorkChop
- What problem we solve for that person

---

## 1) ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Primary ICP
Institutions running **hands-on career pathways** where students must demonstrate practical skill progression, portfolio evidence, and job readiness:
- K-12 CTE districts and regional vocational centers
- Community colleges
- Technical colleges
- Workforce development boards/program operators
- Continuing education and adult upskilling providers

### Program Types We Fit Best
- Skilled trades (culinary, plumbing, electrical, HVAC, construction, automotive, manufacturing, logistics, machining)
- Applied career pathways with lab/shop environments
- Programs needing employer-aligned artifacts, competency evidence, and faster instructional workflows

### Buying Triggers (Signals to Prospect)
- "Our LMS is too generic for lab/shop/trade instruction"
- "Instructors spend too much time on grading/admin"
- "Students need better at-home/off-site practice workflows"
- "We need stronger workforce outcomes and proof-of-skill"
- "We need to work with our existing LMS ecosystem (LTI 1.3)"

## 2) Buyer Personas + Messaging Grid

## Persona A: CTE Director / District Career Pathways Leader (K-12)

**Role focus:** Program quality, pathway outcomes, budget stewardship, district-wide implementation.

**Value Prop (say this):**
"PorkChop gives your district a trades-first learning layer that fits how CTE is actually taught—hands-on, competency-based, and workforce-aligned—while integrating with your LMS strategy."

**How they leverage PorkChop:**
- Standardize pathway delivery across schools/sites
- Provide students structured skill progression and evidence capture
- Use discipline-specific workflows instead of generic assignment tools
- Support CTE teachers with practical tools that reduce admin friction

**Problem solved for this persona:**
- Generic LMS workflows don't map well to trade instruction
- Inconsistent program delivery across multiple campuses
- Weak visibility into practical competency progression
- Pressure to show postsecondary/employment readiness outcomes

**Talk track (short):**
"You don't need to rip up your pathway model—you need better tooling for delivery. PorkChop helps CTE teams run consistent, workforce-aligned programs without forcing academics-first workflows onto hands-on instruction."

---

## Persona B: Dean / VP / Chair (Community or Technical College)

**Role focus:** Enrollment outcomes, completion, workforce placement, institutional efficiency, program differentiation.

**Value Prop (say this):**
"PorkChop helps your career programs improve student readiness and operational efficiency by replacing generic delivery friction with trade-specific learning workflows."

**How they leverage PorkChop:**
- Deliver trade programs with discipline-specific module structure
- Track practical learning artifacts/portfolios for employability evidence
- Improve faculty workflow efficiency in instruction and feedback loops
- Integrate into enterprise LMS environment via LTI 1.3/Advantage

**Problem solved for this persona:**
- Difficult to prove job-ready outcomes with generic LMS artifacts
- Faculty time lost to manual/granular workflow overhead
- Need to modernize programs without disrupting enterprise LMS policy
- Competitive pressure from peer institutions offering stronger applied-tech experiences

**Talk track (short):**
"This is not another generic LMS add-on. It's a workforce-first layer that helps your institution keep LMS continuity while improving how trade programs are delivered and measured."

---

## Persona C: Program Director / Department Lead (Trade Program Owner)

**Role focus:** Instructor execution, student skill progression, day-to-day program performance.

**Value Prop (say this):**
"PorkChop is built around how your instructors teach and assess practical skills, so your team spends less time fighting the platform and more time developing students."

**How they leverage PorkChop:**
- Organize instruction using discipline-native modules (workspace/notebook/community/school)
- Run repeatable practical-learning workflows aligned to real trade tasks
- Support student progression with AI-assisted learning support
- Use program-specific terminology/branding that matches trade identity

**Problem solved for this persona:**
- Current platform feels like adapting trade education to an academic template
- Instructors manually patch together tools for practical instruction
- Students disengage when tooling feels irrelevant to trade work
- Program teams need consistent, reusable workflows

**Talk track (short):**
"Your instructors should not have to translate trade education into 'English 101 workflow.' PorkChop gives them trade-native structure out of the box."

---

## Persona D: Workforce Development / Continuing-Ed Operator

**Role focus:** Speed-to-deployment, employer alignment, adult learner engagement, measurable outcomes.

**Value Prop (say this):**
"PorkChop helps workforce and continuing-ed teams launch and run employer-aligned technical training faster, with practical workflows built for adult learners and skills progression."

**How they leverage PorkChop:**
- Stand up workforce cohorts quickly with configurable discipline shells
- Deliver short-cycle, skills-first training paths
- Create clearer evidence of readiness for employers/partners
- Align learning operations to regional workforce needs

**Problem solved for this persona:**
- Slow implementation cycles for new programs
- Disconnected tools across instruction, practice, and outcomes reporting
- Difficulty proving practical readiness to employers/funders
- Need to scale training beyond one-off instructor heroics

**Talk track (short):**
"If your mandate is workforce outcomes, you need a platform designed for skill execution and progression—not just content hosting."

---

## Persona E: IT / LMS Administrator / Instructional Systems Lead

**Role focus:** Security, interoperability, implementation risk, support load.

**Value Prop (say this):**
"PorkChop is integration-ready for institutional ecosystems, with LTI 1.3/Advantage workflows that reduce adoption friction while enabling trade-specific learning experiences."

**How they leverage PorkChop:**
- Connect with LMS environment using LTI 1.3/Advantage setup flows
- Map claims/fields for operational continuity
- Provision integration tokens for deep linking, roster sync, and grade passback
- Support academic units without adding fragmented, hard-to-manage tools

**Problem solved for this persona:**
- Concern about introducing non-standard tools
- Friction from manual data handoffs and roster workflows
- Pressure to support innovation with minimal risk
- Need for sustainable integrations that don't increase long-term support burden

**Talk track (short):**
"PorkChop gives your institution a trade-focused experience layer without requiring IT to abandon interoperability standards."

## 3) Fast Positioning Statements Sellers Can Use

### One-liner
"PorkChop is a trades-first workforce learning platform that helps institutions deliver hands-on programs with better operational flow and stronger readiness outcomes."

### Two-liner
"Most LMS tools were built for generic academic delivery. PorkChop is built for technical pathways—so K-12 CTE, colleges, and workforce programs can run skill-based education the way it is actually taught and assessed."

### Integration-safe line (for IT-sensitive conversations)
"You can keep your institutional LMS strategy and add a purpose-built trade education layer through LTI 1.3/Advantage workflows."

---

## 4) Discovery Questions (So Sellers Don't Sound Scripted)

Use 3-5 per call:
1. "Where does your current LMS break down for hands-on or lab/shop instruction?"
2. "Which part of instructor workflow is most inefficient today?"
3. "How are you currently documenting proof-of-skill for employers/accreditors?"
4. "How fast can you launch a new workforce pathway right now?"
5. "What would make your program feel clearly workforce-first rather than academic-first?"
6. "How important is LMS interoperability and LTI continuity in your environment?"

---

## 5) Objection Reframes (Simple + Direct)

**"We already have an LMS."**
"That makes sense—most institutions do. PorkChop is the trade-focused delivery layer that improves practical instruction while respecting your LMS strategy."

**"This sounds like a lot to implement."**
"The model is designed to reduce friction: standardized discipline structure, configurable workflows, and LTI-based interoperability for institutional fit."

**"Our programs are unique."**
"Exactly why this works: PorkChop supports discipline-native experiences and can adapt across multiple technical pathways, not just one program type."

**"We need measurable workforce outcomes."**
"That's core to the platform position: practical progression, operational consistency, and stronger evidence of readiness for employer-facing programs."

---

## 6) What to Send After the First Call

Send a short recap with:
1. Their top 2-3 pain points (in their words)
2. The matching persona value prop above
3. A clear "how they leverage PorkChop" summary
4. One implementation-safe line about LTI/interoperability (if relevant)
5. Next step: 20-minute focused demo mapped to their program type

---

## 7) Internal Seller Reminder

Do not rely on generic talking points. Anchor every outreach to:
1. **Who they are (persona)**
2. **What they are trying to achieve (outcome)**
3. **Where current tooling fails (pain)**
4. **How PorkChop operationally fixes that pain (leverage)**

When this is done well, outreach sounds consultative, not scripted.
