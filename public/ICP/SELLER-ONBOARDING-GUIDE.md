## PLATFORM DEEP DIVE

### **What Schools Get with PorkChop**

#### **1. Video Grading System ⭐ BIGGEST SELLER**

**The Problem:**
- Instructors download 30+ video files
- Watch locally in VLC/QuickTime
- Write feedback in separate document
- Upload feedback back to LMS
- Takes 3-5 hours per assignment

**The PorkChop Solution:**
- Embedded video playback in gradebook
- Add timestamps for technique feedback
- Grade while watching (no downloading)
- Students see feedback immediately
- Saves 50% grading time

**Demo Tip:** Show this FIRST in every demo. Instructors will immediately see the value.

---

#### **2. Ingredient Scanner + Recipe Matcher**

**The Problem:**
- Students can't practice at home
- No tools for inventory management
- Meal planning is theoretical
- Ingredient knowledge is memorization

**The PorkChop Solution:**
- Google Vision API scans fridge/pantry
- Auto-categorizes ingredients
- Recipe matcher algorithm finds recipes they can make
- Teaches inventory management hands-on
- Students practice at home without kitchen equipment

**Demo Tip:** Scan your own fridge live. Schools love seeing real-world application.

---

#### **3. Nutrition Tracking (USDA API)**

**The Problem:**
- Nutrition science is taught with textbooks
- Students manually calculate macros
- No hands-on practice
- Time-consuming and error-prone

**The PorkChop Solution:**
- USDA API auto-calculates nutrition for any recipe
- 11 key nutrients tracked
- Health tags (Heart Healthy, Low Glycemic, etc.)
- Per-serving adjustments
- Teaches nutrition science hands-on

**Demo Tip:** Show how fast it calculates nutrition vs manual calculation.

---

#### **4. Global Test Kitchen (Entrepreneurship Simulator)**

**The Problem:**
- Students learn business theory
- No practice running a food business
- Graduate without entrepreneurship experience
- Can't test ideas before investing money

**The PorkChop Solution:**
- Students run mock pop-ups via livestream
- Practice menu planning and presentation
- Build audience before graduation
- Video portfolio of live cooking sessions
- Real-world entrepreneurship practice

**Demo Tip:** Show mock live sessions. Explain this is like a "flight simulator for food entrepreneurs."

---

#### **5. Permanent Student Portfolios**

**The Problem:**
- Assignments disappear after graduation
- Students have no proof of skills
- Employers want to see work, not just resumes
- Design students have Behance, culinary students have nothing

**The PorkChop Solution:**
- Every technique video is part of permanent portfolio
- Public profiles students can share with employers
- Video progression (beginner → advanced)
- Like "Behance for chefs"

**Demo Tip:** Show anonymized student portfolios from pilot schools.

---

#### **6. Market Directory (Google Places API)**

**The Problem:**
- Students don't know how to source ingredients
- Market research is theoretical
- No practice with vendor relationships
- Graduate without supplier knowledge

**The PorkChop Solution:**
- Google Places API finds local suppliers
- 7 categories (Seafood, Meat, Produce, Farms, Equipment, etc.)
- Distance calculations and sorting
- Excludes big box retailers (Walmart, Costco)
- Real-world vendor sourcing practice

**Demo Tip:** Search for markets near their school. Makes it immediately relevant.

### **Step 1: Lead Prospecting & Research**

**How to Find Leads (30 minutes per school):**

#### **A. Find the School's Culinary Program**
1. Go to school's website
2. Search for: "culinary program" OR "culinary arts" OR "hospitality" OR "food service"
3. Navigate to the program page (usually under Academics → Programs)
4. Look for program details, faculty listings, contact information

#### **B. Identify Decision Makers**
**Who to target (in priority order):**
1. **Program Director** / **Department Chair** (BEST)
2. **Culinary Program Coordinator**
3. **Lead Culinary Instructor** / **Chef Instructor**
4. **Dean of Career & Technical Education** (community colleges)
5. **Director of Culinary Education** (top-tier schools)

**Where to find them:**
- Program page → "Faculty & Staff" section
- "Meet Our Team" or "Our Instructors" pages
- "Contact Us" section
- School directory (search by department)

#### **C. Get Contact Information**
**From School Website:**
- Direct email (usually firstname.lastname@school.edu)
- Office phone number
- Department phone number

**If not listed on website:**
- Try standard format: firstname.lastname@school.edu
- Call main number, ask for culinary department
- Check LinkedIn for their profile (often lists email)

#### **D. LinkedIn Research**
1. Search: "[Name] [School Name] culinary"
2. Send connection request with note:
   ```
   Hi [First Name], I work with culinary programs like [School Name]'s 
   and wanted to connect. Looking forward to learning more about your program!
   ```
3. Check their profile for:
   - How long they've been there
   - Previous schools (reference in outreach)
   - Shared connections
   - Recent posts (engagement opportunities)

#### **E. Add to HubSpot**
**Required fields:**
- Full name
- Title
- School name
- Email
- Phone
- LinkedIn URL
- Tier (1, 2, or 3)
- Notes (program size, recent news, pain points)

**Set reminders:**
- Follow-up date (3 days after first contact)
- Next action (call, email, LinkedIn)

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### **"We already have an LMS and it works fine."**

**Response:**
"Totally get it. Question: How do your instructors currently grade video technique submissions? Are they downloading 30 files and watching them locally, or can they do it in-platform with timestamps? That's the kind of thing PorkChop fixes. Can I show you a quick comparison?"

**Why this works:** Exposes the pain point they didn't realize they had.

---

### **"We don't have budget for new software."**

**Response:**
"Actually, PorkChop replaces your current LMS—so it's cost-neutral. You're already paying $15-40 per student. Same budget, just shifted to one that's purpose-built for culinary instead of generic. Want to see a pricing comparison?"

**Why this works:** Removes budget objection. It's a switch, not an addition.

---

### **"Our faculty are comfortable with our current system."**

**Response:**
"Absolutely, and the interface is similar—it's still an LMS. But imagine if your culinary instructors had video grading tools that actually work, and students had ingredient scanners for practice. Would they be excited about that? Let me show you what other schools are doing—takes 10 minutes."

**Why this works:** Acknowledges their concern, then reframes as an upgrade, not a replacement.

---

### **"I need to talk to IT/Dean/Department first."**

**Response:**
"Makes total sense. What if I send you a 3-minute demo video you can forward to them? Then if there's interest, we can do a live walkthrough together. Does that work?"

**Why this works:** Gives them something to share internally, keeps momentum going.

---

### **"Send me some information."**

**Response:**
"Happy to. Before I do—what's the biggest pain point your culinary instructors have right now? Is it grading video assignments? Limited practice time? Skills assessment? I'll send you exactly what's relevant."

**[Get the pain point, then]:**
"Great, I'll send that over today. Can we schedule 15 minutes next week after you've reviewed it?"

**Why this works:** Gets you a commitment before sending info. Prevents "black hole" of no response.

---

### **"This sounds too complicated to implement."**

**Response:**
"Actually, implementation is super simple. Students sign up with their school email, instructors get a 1-hour training, and we handle all the technical setup. Most schools are up and running in 1 week. Want to see the onboarding process?"

**Why this works:** Removes implementation fear. Shows it's turnkey.

---

### **"What if students don't use it?"**

**Response:**
"Great question. Students love PorkChop because it has tools they actually use—ingredient scanner, recipe matcher, nutrition tracking. It's not just another place to upload assignments. Pilot schools report 85%+ weekly active usage. Want to see student testimonials?"

**Why this works:** Shows proof of engagement. Students actually want to use it.

---

### **"We need to see results first."**

**Response:**
"Totally fair. What if I show you what [Competitor School] students are building? Anonymized portfolios, real technique videos, nutrition projects. 15 minutes—no commitment. Does Thursday work?"

**Why this works:** Offers social proof without requiring commitment.
