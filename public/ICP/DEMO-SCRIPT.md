## DEMO STRUCTURE (20 minutes total)

---

## PART 1: INTRO (2 minutes)

### **Opening (30 seconds)**

"Hi [First Name]! Thanks for taking the time today. I'm [Your Name] from PorkChop. Before we dive in, I want to make sure this is valuable for you. 

We have 20 minutes, and I'm going to show you three things:
1. How instructors grade video technique submissions in half the time
2. How students practice at home with our ingredient scanner
3. How students build entrepreneurship skills with Global Test Kitchen

But first, I want to hear from you."

---

### **Discovery Questions (90 seconds)**

**Ask 2-3 of these based on their tier:**

**For Community Colleges:**
- "What LMS platform are you currently using?"
- "How do your instructors currently grade video assignments?"
- "What's the biggest challenge with limited kitchen space?"
- "How do students practice skills at home?"

**For Top-Tier Schools:**
- "What do your students show employers when they graduate?"
- "How do you differentiate from other culinary schools?"
- "Do students have a portfolio of their work?"
- "What entrepreneurship training do you offer?"

**For Mid-Market Universities:**
- "What tools do students use for capstone projects?"
- "How do you teach business/entrepreneurship skills?"
- "What market research tools do students have access to?"

**LISTEN TO THEIR ANSWERS. Take notes. Reference them throughout demo.**

---

## PART 2: THE DEMO (12 minutes)

---

### **FEATURE 1: Video Grading System (5 minutes) ⭐ MOST IMPORTANT**

**Setup (30 seconds):**
"You mentioned [reference their pain point about video grading]. Let me show you how PorkChop solves this."

**Demo Steps:**

1. **Show Gradebook Modal (30 seconds)**
   - "This is the gradebook. It looks like a book—fits the culinary aesthetic."
   - Click to open gradebook
   - Show list of assignments (Week 3: Knife Skills, Week 5: Sauces, Week 7: Proteins)

2. **Open Assignment (30 seconds)**
   - Click "Week 3: French Knife Skills & Mother Sauces"
   - Show assignment details (100 points, 15% weight, techniques required)
   - "Instructors create assignments with specific techniques to assess."

3. **Show Student Submissions (1 minute)**
   - Click through 2-3 student submissions
   - Show video thumbnails
   - "Students upload videos directly. No downloading required."

4. **Grade a Video (2 minutes) ⭐ THE MONEY SHOT**
   - Click play on a video
   - "Watch this—I can grade while watching."
   - Play video for 10-15 seconds
   - Pause at technique moment
   - Add timestamp feedback: "0:23 - Great knife grip! Thumb and forefinger on blade."
   - Resume video, pause again
   - Add another timestamp: "0:45 - Watch your finger position on the claw."
   - "Students see exactly where in the video you're commenting."

5. **Assign Grade (30 seconds)**
   - Select letter grade (A-)
   - Enter numeric score (92)
   - Add overall feedback in notepad
   - Click "Save Grade"
   - "Done. No downloading, no separate documents, no uploading feedback."

**The Close (30 seconds):**
"Compare this to downloading 30 video files, watching in VLC, writing feedback in Word, and uploading it back. This saves instructors 50% of their grading time. Does this solve the problem you mentioned?"

**[PAUSE FOR THEIR REACTION. Let them respond.]**

---

### **FEATURE 2: Ingredient Scanner + Recipe Matcher (4 minutes)**

**Setup (30 seconds):**
"Now let me show you how students practice at home—even if they don't have a full kitchen."

**Demo Steps:**

1. **Show MyKitchen Module (30 seconds)**
   - Navigate to MyKitchen
   - "This is the student's digital cupboard."
   - Show jar shelf display with ingredients

2. **Scan Ingredients (1 minute) ⭐ LIVE DEMO**
   - Click "Scan Ingredients"
   - Hold up your phone with a pre-loaded photo (or use webcam on real food)
   - "I'm using Google Vision API to scan my fridge."
   - Show detected ingredients appearing
   - "It auto-categorizes—vegetables, proteins, dairy, etc."
   - Click "Add to Kitchen"

3. **Show Recipe Matcher (1.5 minutes)**
   - Click "Find Recipes"
   - "Now watch this—our algorithm matches recipes to what they have."
   - Show recipe results appearing
   - Point out match scores
   - "This recipe scores high because they have 8 of 10 ingredients."
   - Click on a recipe
   - Show full recipe with ingredients highlighted (green = have, red = need)

4. **Explain the Algorithm (30 seconds)**
   - "The algorithm considers:
     - Ingredients they have
     - Their kitchen setup (dorm vs full kitchen)
     - Their experience level (beginner vs advanced)
     - Equipment requirements"
   - "It teaches inventory management, meal planning, and ingredient knowledge—all at home."

**The Close (30 seconds):**
"Students can practice this at home, on weekends, anytime. No kitchen equipment needed. They're building real skills outside of class time. How would this help with your [reference their challenge about limited kitchen space]?"

**[PAUSE FOR THEIR REACTION.]**

---

### **FEATURE 3: Global Test Kitchen (3 minutes)**

**Setup (30 seconds):**
"Last thing—let me show you how students practice entrepreneurship before they graduate."

**Demo Steps:**

1. **Navigate to Chef's Corner (15 seconds)**
   - Click "Chef's Corner"
   - "This is our entrepreneurship module."

2. **Show Global Test Kitchen (1 minute)**
   - Click "Global Test Kitchen"
   - Show mock live sessions
   - "Students can go live and run mock pop-ups or catering events."
   - Point out viewer counts
   - "They practice:
     - Menu planning
     - Presentation skills
     - Time management
     - Building an audience"

3. **Show Scheduling (30 seconds)**
   - Click "Schedule Session"
   - Show scheduling form (dish, cuisine, date, time, session type)
   - "They can schedule future sessions, just like a real business."

4. **Show Market Directory (45 seconds)**
   - Click "Market Directory"
   - "For market research, we integrate Google Places API."
   - Show categories (Seafood, Meat, Produce, Farms, Equipment)
   - Click "Seafood Markets"
   - Show results with distances
   - "Students learn to source from real vendors, compare pricing, build supplier relationships."

**The Close (30 seconds):**
"This is like a flight simulator for food entrepreneurs. They practice running a business before investing real money. They graduate with video portfolios, market research experience, and entrepreneurship skills. How does this fit with your program goals?"

**[PAUSE FOR THEIR REACTION.]**

## PART 3: Q&A (3 minutes)

### **Invite Questions**
"Those are the three big features. What questions do you have?"

**[LISTEN. Answer honestly. If you don't know, say "Great question—let me get you an answer by EOD."]**

---

### **Common Questions & Answers**

**"How much does this cost?"**
"PorkChop is priced the same as traditional LMS platforms—$15-40 per student depending on your school size and contract length. It's cost-neutral because it replaces your current LMS, not an add-on. Want me to send over a detailed pricing breakdown?"

**"How long does implementation take?"**
"Most schools are up and running in 1 week. Students sign up with their school email, instructors get a 1-hour training session, and we handle all the technical setup. We also offer weekly check-ins for the first month."

**"What if our faculty don't adopt it?"**
"Great question. We've found that when instructors see the video grading system, they immediately want to use it because it saves so much time. Students love it because it has tools they actually use—not just another place to upload assignments. Pilot schools report 85%+ weekly active usage."

**"Can we integrate with our existing systems?"**
"Yes, we have API integration capabilities. What systems are you currently using? [Take notes, follow up with Patrick if needed]"

**"Do you have case studies or references?"**
"Absolutely. We're working with [mention any pilot schools or say 'several community colleges and culinary institutes']. I can connect you with [Reference School] if you'd like to hear directly from them."

**"What about data security and privacy?"**
"All data is encrypted and stored securely. We're FERPA compliant and follow industry best practices for student data protection. I can send you our security documentation."

## DEMO TIPS

### **Do's:**
✅ Smile (they can hear it in your voice)
✅ Use their name frequently
✅ Reference their pain points throughout
✅ Pause for reactions
✅ Ask questions ("Does this solve your problem?")
✅ Show enthusiasm
✅ Take notes during discovery
✅ Screen share (don't just talk)

### **Don'ts:**
❌ Rush through features
❌ Talk over them
❌ Skip discovery questions
❌ Forget to close (always schedule next step)
❌ Bad-mouth competitors
❌ Oversell features they don't need
❌ Wing it (follow this script)
