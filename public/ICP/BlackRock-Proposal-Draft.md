## Program Narrative

### Q1 — The Data: Target Occupations & Demand

This proposal targets skilled-trades occupations tied to Maine's infrastructure and energy transition:
- **Electricians** and **HVAC technicians** — explicitly named RFP priority occupations
- **Construction trades** — Passive House builders, carpenters, weatherization technicians (energy infrastructure)
- **Automotive / EV technicians** — transportation infrastructure
- **Advanced manufacturing / aerospace sheet metal** — supply-chain infrastructure

**Demand evidence:**
- Maine's Economic Development Strategy calls for adding 75,000 workers to the talent pool over 10 years as retirements outpace entries.
- Maine's clean energy mandate (80% renewable electricity by 2030) requires major expansion of electrical, HVAC, and weatherization trades.
- Maine DOL Apprenticeship data: ~11,500 active apprentices statewide in 2025, with documented shortages in non-traditional occupations including electrician, pipefitter, and welder.

### Q3 — Regional Ecosystem

Maine's skilled-trades training ecosystem includes the Maine Department of Labor Apprenticeship Program, the Maine Community College System, Efficiency Maine Trust, FocusMaine, and employer sponsors across infrastructure and energy sectors. Educate Maine, through Maine Career Catalyst, is a registered **Intermediary Sponsor** of apprenticeship with the Maine DOL — meaning it already connects employers, apprentices, and related technical instruction statewide.

What this proposal adds: a shared **technology and data layer (PorkChop)** that standardizes curriculum delivery, credential tracking, and outcome reporting across every employer-sponsored apprenticeship Educate Maine manages — infrastructure the ecosystem currently lacks.

### Q4 — Role in the Skilled Trades Ecosystem

Educate Maine is a **workforce intermediary and direct training provider**. It recruits employers, registers apprenticeships, coordinates related technical instruction, and supports apprentices to completion. PorkChop is the **technology/data platform partner** that increases capacity, readiness, and placement tracking across that work.

### Q5 — Proposed Intervention

**Worker Training:** Expand Educate Maine's registered apprenticeship and pre-apprenticeship programs in infrastructure-aligned trades from ~[FILL: current ~100–150] active apprentices to ~300 over the grant period, across electrical, HVAC, construction/Passive House, and automotive pathways.

**Capacity-Building:** Deploy PorkChop as the statewide learning management and credentialing platform. PorkChop:
- Hosts each employer's related technical instruction (content-agnostic — Educate Maine uploads existing curriculum)
- Tracks apprentice progress, lesson completion, and competency attainment in a single Admin Dashboard
- Issues portable credentials through its Skills Wallet (exports to Maine DOL and state systems)
- Generates the participant-level data required for biannual KPI reporting

### Q6 — Measurable Outcomes (2-Year)

- **300 apprentices/pre-apprentices enrolled** on the platform by end of Year 1
- **[FILL: ~150] participants earning an industry-recognized credential** (journey-level, BPI, OSHA-10, NCCER, Passive House) by end of Year 2
- **25+ employer commitments** to new or expanded apprenticeship slots
- **90% training retention** rate
- **80% placement** in a skilled-trades job within 90 days of completion
- **Wage at placement ≥ 15% above** the regional entry median

### Q7 — Catalytic Impact

The PorkChop platform, once deployed, becomes permanent statewide infrastructure: every future apprenticeship Educate Maine registers can be onboarded at near-zero marginal cost. Credential data flows into Maine DOL and Efficiency Maine workforce pipelines. The model is directly replicable by other Maine intermediaries and Community Action Agencies. Regional assets leveraged: Educate Maine's employer network, Maine DOL apprenticeship registration, FocusMaine funding relationships, and PorkChop's technology platform.

### Q8 — Constraints, Assumptions & Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Rural geographic barriers to instruction | PorkChop delivers related technical instruction remotely; reduces travel burden |
| Employer recruitment for new slots | Educate Maine's existing multi-employer network and Intermediary Sponsor status |
| Post-grant funding gap | Sustainability via employer-paid subscriptions + state apprenticeship funds |
| Partner coordination | Single intermediary structure; PorkChop integrated as one vendor relationship |
| Data/credential standardization | PorkChop enforces consistent competency tracking across all employers |

### Q9 — Program Design

All apprenticeships follow **Maine DOL Registered Apprenticeship** standards: paid on-the-job training plus related technical instruction. PorkChop hosts the related instruction (Electrical, HVAC, Construction, Automotive modules), aligned to industry-recognized credentials (journey-level, BPI, OSHA-10, NCCER, Passive House Tradesperson). Recruitment runs year-round through Educate Maine's employer partners; cohorts start Fall/Winter/Summer. Evidence of effectiveness: Educate Maine's existing sponsored programs at Darling's, Maine Passive House, Juniper Design + Build, PK Floats, and Bangor Savings Bank. ### Q10 — Participant Supports

- Paid apprenticeships (wages during OJT) — primary barrier reducer
- Stipends/participant supports line ($25,000/year) for transportation and tools
- PorkChop Community module for peer support across a rural geography
- PorkChop AI Assistant for on-demand curriculum help between sessions
- Educate Maine workforce partnership staff provide navigation and employer liaison

### Q11 — Sustainability of Outcomes

After the grant, PorkChop transitions to an **employer-funded subscription** ($49/user/month), embedded as a standard cost of Educate Maine's apprenticeship sponsorship. Maine DOL apprenticeship funding and Efficiency Maine clean-energy workforce grants provide continuing public support. The credentialing infrastructure and curriculum library persist permanently. Structural change: standardized, data-backed apprenticeship delivery becomes the default statewide.

### Q14 — Data Collection Methodology

PorkChop is the data collection system. At the participant level it tracks enrollment, lesson/competency completion, credential attainment, and portfolio evidence. The Admin Dashboard aggregates this into real-time analytics; the Skills Wallet records issued credentials. Educate Maine supplements with employer placement and wage surveys at 90 days and 6 months. Biannual KPI reports are generated directly from PorkChop analytics. Post-completion engagement continues via the platform's alumni/community features.
