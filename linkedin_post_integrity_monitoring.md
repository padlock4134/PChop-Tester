## 🛡️ Preventing Cheating in Vocational Education: A Trust-Based Approach

In traditional academic settings, preventing cheating often means invasive proctoring, webcam monitoring, and browser lockdowns. But here's the thing: **that approach doesn't work for skills-based vocational education.**

At PorkChop, we're building a learning platform for trades—culinary arts, automotive repair, manufacturing, HVAC, plumbing, and more. Our students aren't taking multiple-choice tests. They're learning to rebuild engines, wire electrical panels, and weld metal joints. You can't fake those skills.

### 🤔 The Challenge

Still, we needed to address academic integrity. Schools and employers asked: "How do you prevent cheating?"

Our answer? **We don't treat our students like criminals. We treat them like professionals.**

### ✅ Our Lightweight Integrity System

Instead of invasive surveillance, we built a **trust-based integrity monitoring system** with three simple components:

**1. Unusual Completion Pattern Detection**
- Flags accounts completing modules suspiciously fast (e.g., 10+ modules in an hour)
- Detects bot-like behavior (identical completion times)
- Alerts instructors to investigate, not auto-punish

**2. Plagiarism Detection for Written Work**
- Compares text submissions between students
- Calculates similarity scores
- Highlights potential issues for instructor review

**3. Activity Anomaly Alerts**
- Tracks unusual login patterns (multiple IPs in short time)
- Detects account sharing indicators
- Single-session enforcement (one device at a time)

### 🎯 Why This Works for Trades

The reality is simple: **You can't cheat your way to being a skilled tradesperson.**

- If you copied your way through automotive theory, you'll fail when asked to diagnose a real engine problem
- If you plagiarized your welding assignments, your joints will fail inspection
- If you rushed through HVAC modules without learning, you won't pass your certification exam

**The work itself is the ultimate verification.**

### 💡 The Philosophy

Our system:
- ✅ **Flags suspicious behavior** for instructor review
- ✅ **Respects student privacy** (no webcam surveillance)
- ✅ **Trusts instructors' judgment** (alerts, not automatic penalties)
- ✅ **Focuses on practical skills** (hands-on assessments can't be faked)
- ✅ **Maintains academic integrity** without treating students like suspects

### 📊 Early Results

Since implementing this system:
- Instructors appreciate the visibility without the overhead
- Students feel respected, not surveilled
- False positives are minimal (alerts are reviewed, not automated)
- Real issues are caught early and addressed constructively

### 🔑 The Takeaway

**For skills-based education, trust + transparency + accountability > surveillance.**

When you're teaching real-world skills, the best anti-cheating measure is simple: **Make students actually do the work.** Everything else is just noise.
